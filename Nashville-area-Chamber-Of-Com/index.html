Why Working In Nashville?

The Nashville region is home to more than 1.9 million people and more than 40,000 businesses. Middle Tennessee is defined by a diverse economy, low costs of living and doing business, a creative culture and a well-educated population.(source: https://www.nashvillechamber.com/)

NO.1!

Nashville Named #2 Best Place for New Businesses---SmartAdvisor

    Nashville sees only 1% of businesses close due to finances, tied for the third-lowest rate in SmartAdvisor's study. The city also has the second-highest percentage of all businesses receiving a business loan from a bank or financial institution - at approximately 20% - and the third highest percentage of businesses receiving at least $100,000 from a bank or financial institution, at a little more than 17%.

NO.2!

Nashville Named #3 Best City for Young Professionals---SmartAsset

    SmartAsset ranked cities based on nine metrics that encompassed both economic and livability factors relevant to young professionals including cost of living, entertainment, job diversity and income, along with the unemployment rate for those between 25 and 34. Nashville was previously ranked #20 in the 2018 study, but climbed to the top of the rankings due to a $16,000 increase in median earnings from 2016-2017.

NO.3

Nashville #7 Large Metro Gaining the Most Young Professionals---Avalanche

    During the past decade, talent has become the most important driver of economic vibrancy. Having a growing pool of talent is especially important in an era when labor is scarce and more difficult to attract. According to the record, the Nashville city was ranked as the #7 large metro gaining the most young professionals (ages 25-34) based on 2018 U.S. Census Bureau data. Nashville was previously ranked #37 in 2017.

Why Living In Nashville?

According to the 2000 census bureau, Nashville has a population of 569,891, of which 66.99% are white, 25.92% are African American and 2.33% are Asian American. More than 100,000 American expatriates live in Nashville, which is home to a large Hispanic, middle eastern, south Asian and southeast Asian population. There are 100 languages spoken there. REQUIREMENTS & BENEFITS

What we need?
• Education background: Graduate student or graduated student with any major of Civil Engineering, Construction Administration, Data Analytics, Computer Science, Marketing, Advertising or any major related to Engineering or Management.
• Work experience: Relative work experience, internship or project participation is preferred.
• Age:23-29 years old is preferred.
